Legal Information & FAQ

Below, you'll find answers to frequently asked questions about the required information and legal actions to take in the case of a missing person.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the required information for the missing person?

To effectively search for a missing person, the following details are essential:

  • Conscious State: Identify if the person is conscious, unconscious, or experiencing parallel or blind states.
  • Gender: Specify if the person is male or female.
  • Age: Young (From birth to 18 years old.) / Adult (18+).
  • Location: Provide their birthplace or current location.
  • Life Conditions:
    • Any personal conflicts or issues they might have faced.
    • Known fears or vulnerabilities.
  • Governorship: Identify the person responsible for their care or decisions.
  • Literacy: Determine if the person can read or write.
What is the required information for login?

To access the platform securely, users are required to provide the following information:

  • National ID: A government-issued ID is required to verify your identity.
  • Standard Login Details: This includes your email and password for account access.
What are the legal actions to take after missing someone?

Legal procedures and actions can vary by country. Here's a general guide, with specific details for Egypt:

  • In Egypt: Authorities, such as the police, typically require a waiting period of 48 hours after the individual’s disappearance before initiating a search. During this time, it’s crucial to:
    • Search our platform for any potential matches.
    • Check all social media platforms for posts or sightings.
    • Visit nearby hospitals and police stations for any records or sightings.
  • After 48 Hours: Escalate the matter to the police or relevant authorities. Provide them with all available details and proof of the person’s disappearance.
